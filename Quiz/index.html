Life and Works of Ādi Shankarāchārya

Ādi Shankarāchārya was one of the greatest philosophers and theologians of ancient India, the most renowned exponent of the Advaita Vedanta school of philosophy, from whose doctrines several of the main currents of modern Indian thought are derived. He wrote commentaries on several texts like the Brahma-sutra, the principal Upanishads, and the Bhagavad Gīta. He played a pivotal role in re-establishing vedic rituals and practices in India. This quiz is about the life, the works and greatness of Ādi Shankarāchārya and intends to spread awareness about the same with an objective to educate and engage people in learning more about our ancient ideals that help in forming an ideal, responsible and enlightened society.
