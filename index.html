FARSHID DARUWALLA : Our country has a lot of potential which when tapped correctly can produce fabulous results. As history has it, India has been able to enthrall anyone who sets eyes on her. It is quite the privilege to be part of such a vibrant and a distinct country. Amidst modernization and technological advancements, we the youth of the country have forgotten our roots and our rich heritage. Keeping this in mind Sthaapna organized a research conclave where the focus was: Lost Indian Businesses. Surprisingly the conclave turned into a melting pot of various intricate art techniques and forms which indeed were quite intriguing. From gara embroidery, patola sarees and architecture to trade relations between India and China. Sthaapna played quite a role in making the youth understand what they have been missing out on all this while. When it comes to me, I chose the Gara Embroidery which is highly intricate and delicate and has a beauty of its own. One interprets it differently every single time as each piece has a different story to tell. This research conclave not only made me dig deeper into our country's art forms but also made me connect with my own heritage. Going through different gara pieces of my own family to understanding how one is actually made, this research has definitely taught me more than I expected. Lastly, Sthaapna has presented a fabulous conclave where they successfully completed their goal of making today's youth realize how vibrant and beautiful our past is.
