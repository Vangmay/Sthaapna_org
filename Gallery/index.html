Conclave One

RESUSCITATING OUR DYING HERITAGE India, a melting pot of cultures was once called “Sone Ki Chidiya” owing to its prosperous trade and socio-economic systems. Unfortunately, due to changing times many of such trades and practices have become defunct. Ranging from intricate art forms to simple textile weaving, these businesses are teetering on the brink of extinction. As citizens of this rich land it is our duty to revive what is a rather important part of our heritage and culture. With a view of reviving, Sthaapna presents its “LOST INDIAN BUSINESSES AND THEIR REVIVAL” conclave, drawing attention to the various obsolete professions that have now lost their glory and are fighting for survival. Being held on 26th January, this research conclave calls for young students to present research papers on Indian businesses that have become inert and have lost their significance. It is our rich heritage and a treasure which has been protected for us by our ancestors. We ought to take pride in its meaning and importance.

Reviving the lost philosophy of Great Indian Sages

For a long period of time, our country, India was under the influence of foreign invaders. A handful of marauders decided what we Indians would learn of our past. There was a time when this holy land treasured scholarly gems like Adi Shankaracharya, who gave the philosophy of Advaita Vedanta; Kabir Das, whose writings inspired the Bhakti Movement; and Guru Samarth Ramdas, who inspired valorous kings like Chhatrapati Shivaji. When Thomas Babington Macaulay commented that a single shelf of a good European library was worth the whole native literature of India and Arabia, it was an attempt to erode the legacy of our rich literature and substantial knowledge. An attempt to never let the aboriginal people take pride in their literature, to never let the discoveries and philosophies be recognized with the originators. Despite the efforts to mock and defame the sublime lives of the great Indian Saints, their unparalleled philosophy could not be entirely eliminated. In the contemporary world, it has been accepted that the Indus Valley Civilization was a prosperous and highly developed civilization. The Indian subcontinent had witnessed the progenitors of wisdom, enlightening the human race with the wisdom of Vedas. While the earliest of the civilizations were still learning to question the human existence, the great Indian seers had already discovered the laws that govern the emergence and evolution of universe. The sages and seers not only gave spiritual insights but they also established a strong tradition of science and technology with some ground-breaking discoveries and innovation.
