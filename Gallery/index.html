Conclave One

RESUSCITATING OUR DYING HERITAGE India, a melting pot of cultures was once called “Sone Ki Chidiya” owing to its prosperous trade and socio-economic systems. Unfortunately, due to changing times many of such trades and practices have become defunct. Ranging from intricate art forms to simple textile weaving, these businesses are teetering on the brink of extinction. As citizens of this rich land it is our duty to revive what is a rather important part of our heritage and culture. With a view of reviving, Sthaapna presents its “LOST INDIAN BUSINESSES AND THEIR REVIVAL” conclave, drawing attention to the various obsolete professions that have now lost their glory and are fighting for survival. Being held on 26th January, this research conclave calls for young students to present research papers on Indian businesses that have become inert and have lost their significance. It is our rich heritage and a treasure which has been protected for us by our ancestors. We ought to take pride in its meaning and importance.
