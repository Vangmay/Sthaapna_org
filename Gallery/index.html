Conclave One

Our 1st Research Conclave was organized on the occasion of Republic Day, on 26th January, 2021. The theme of the conclave was “The Lost Indian Businesses and Their Revival”. The aim of this research conclave was to educate students about small indigenous businesses that have now lost their significance. This research conclave called for young students to present research papers on Indian businesses that have become inert and have lost their glory, with an emphasis on the possibilities of their revival. A guest Lecture was organized prior to the conclave, on 24th January, 2021. The Speaker for the session was Mr. Jai Mrug, and he spoke on the “Historical Aspects of Indian Entrepreneurship”. The Guest Speaker for the inaugural session of the event was Dr. Vikrant Singh Tomar, and he spoke about the glorious history of our nation and motivated the participants to keep spreading awareness about our rich heritage with perseverance and determination. The Judges for the conclave were Dr. Pankaj Mishra, Mr. Jai Mrug, Mr. Sanjiva Shivesh, Mr. Abinash Kumar, Prof. Rakesh Upadhyay, Ms. Ashima Mahajan and Prof. Yogesh Kamath. Number of registrations - 42 Number of participants - 11 The winners were –

  • Tanisha Acharya, securing the 1st Prize.
  • Sharmishtha Ranawat, securing the 2nd Prize
  • Moaz Bin Asad, securing the 3rd Prize
  • Farshid Daruwalla, securing the “Best Research Report” Prize
